Work Packages

GUIDE-AI is structured into seven work packages covering the full research lifecycle — from clinical requirements and AI development to validation, ethics, and dissemination.

All Work Packages

WP01

Project Management & Coordination

Lead: [Institution TBD]

WP01 ensures the smooth running of the entire GUIDE-AI project. This includes financial management, risk monitoring, internal communication, consortium agreement administration, and reporting to the IHI JU. It also oversees quality assurance across all work packages and facilitates cross-WP coordination through regular steering committee meetings.

Key Activities

  • ✦ Consortium governance and stakeholder communication
  • ✦ Financial management and IHI reporting
  • ✦ Risk register and mitigation planning
  • ✦ Quality assurance and data management plan
WP02

Clinical Requirements & Knowledge Engineering

Lead: [Institution TBD]

WP02 defines the clinical requirements for Guideline Navigators through co-design with clinicians and patients. It curates and structures clinical practice guideline corpora for heart failure, CKD, COPD, asthma, and IBD, and builds the ontological framework needed for cross-disease reasoning.

Key Activities

  • ✦ Clinician and patient co-design workshops
  • ✦ Guideline corpus curation (HF, CKD, COPD, Asthma, IBD)
  • ✦ Clinical ontology and comorbidity framework
  • ✦ Benchmark question-answer dataset creation
WP03

Data Governance & Infrastructure

Lead: [Institution TBD]

WP03 establishes the GDPR-compliant data infrastructure for GUIDE-AI — including federated learning architecture, secure data processing pipelines, and the data management plan. It ensures that clinical data used for model evaluation is handled in compliance with EU data protection regulation.

Key Activities

  • ✦ GDPR-compliant data management plan
  • ✦ Federated infrastructure design and deployment
  • ✦ Secure annotation and evaluation pipelines
  • ✦ Data access and sharing agreements
WP04

AI & LLM Development

Lead: [Institution TBD]

WP04 is the technical core of GUIDE-AI. It covers the selection, fine-tuning, and alignment of large language models on clinical guideline content. Key methods include retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), instruction tuning, and RLHF. The WP also develops hallucination detection modules and the safety guardrail system.

Key Activities

  • ✦ LLM selection and baseline benchmarking
  • ✦ RAG pipeline development and optimisation
  • ✦ Instruction tuning and RLHF on guideline QA
  • ✦ Hallucination detection and safety guardrails
WP05

Clinical Validation & Real-World Testing

Lead: [Institution TBD]

WP05 translates the AI-developed Guideline Navigator into clinical reality. It designs and executes prospective evaluation studies at partner clinical sites across Europe, measuring the impact on guideline adherence, decision quality, clinician workflow integration, and patient outcomes.

Key Activities

  • ✦ Clinical study design and ethics approval
  • ✦ EHR integration at partner sites
  • ✦ Prospective adherence and outcome measurement
  • ✦ Usability and clinician experience evaluation
WP06

Ethics, Regulatory Affairs & Patient Involvement

Lead: [Institution TBD]

WP06 ensures that GUIDE-AI tools are developed and deployed in an ethically robust and legally compliant manner. It covers EU AI Act classification, CE marking pathway analysis, patient and public involvement (PPI), and bias auditing across all disease areas and demographic groups.

Key Activities

  • ✦ EU AI Act compliance and regulatory strategy
  • ✦ Ethics framework and institutional approvals
  • ✦ Patient & Public Involvement (PPI) programme
  • ✦ Bias auditing and fairness evaluation
WP07

Dissemination, Communication & Impact

Lead: [Institution TBD]

WP07 manages all external communication, scientific dissemination, and stakeholder engagement activities for GUIDE-AI. This includes maintaining the project website (this site), publishing open-access research, presenting at scientific conferences, engaging policymakers, and preparing a sustainability plan for post-project impact.

Key Activities

  • ✦ Project website and social media management
  • ✦ Open-access scientific publications
  • ✦ Conference presentations and public events
  • ✦ Policy engagement and sustainability planning

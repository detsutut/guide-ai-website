About GUIDE-AI

Understanding the challenge, the project, and the scientific approach behind AI-powered guideline navigation.

Background

The problem with clinical guidelines

Clinical practice guidelines (CPGs) represent the gold standard of evidence-based medicine. Produced by leading professional societies — ESC, KDIGO, GOLD, GINA — they synthesise thousands of studies into graded recommendations designed to improve patient outcomes.

Yet applying them in practice is far from straightforward. A single patient with heart failure, chronic kidney disease, and COPD is served by three separate guideline documents running to hundreds of pages each. No single guideline addresses the intersection. Clinicians must synthesise this complexity mentally, in real time, under pressure.

The result: significant variation in guideline adherence, avoidable adverse events, and inequalities in access to evidence-based care — particularly outside specialist centres.

What We Aim to Achieve

Project Objectives

GUIDE-AI targets five interconnected objectives spanning technology development, clinical translation, and scientific dissemination.

1

Build validated Guideline Navigators

Develop and benchmark LLM-based Guideline Navigators for heart failure, CKD, COPD, asthma, and IBD — capable of answering free-text clinical questions with evidence-graded, traceable responses.

2

Enable cross-disease reasoning

Create an ontological framework that allows the Guideline Navigator to handle patients with multiple comorbidities, integrating recommendations from different disease-specific guidelines.

3

Demonstrate clinical safety and effectiveness

Prospectively evaluate Guideline Navigators in real clinical workflows at multiple EU sites, measuring impact on guideline adherence, clinical decision quality, and clinician efficiency.

4

Address ethics, safety, and regulation

Develop a rigorous framework for AI safety in clinical guideline applications — addressing hallucination risk, bias, explainability requirements, and the EU AI Act classification of GUIDE-AI tools.

5

Maximise scientific and societal impact

Publish findings in open-access journals, share validated benchmarks and datasets, engage patient organisations and policymakers, and establish a sustainability pathway for the Guideline Navigator beyond the project lifetime.

Scientific & Technical Approach

How GUIDE-AI works

A four-layer architecture connects clinical knowledge, AI reasoning, safety assurance, and clinical integration.

Layer 1

Guideline Knowledge Base

Clinical practice guidelines from ESC, KDIGO, GOLD, GINA, and ECCO are systematically curated, structured, and annotated. Recommendations are tagged with evidence grades, population descriptors, and comorbidity flags.

Layer 2

LLM Adaptation & Alignment

Open-weight and proprietary LLMs are fine-tuned using supervised learning and reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF) on clinical question-answer pairs grounded in guideline content. Hallucination detection and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) ensure answer fidelity.

Layer 3

Safety & Explainability Framework

Every model output is evaluated by an automated safety layer that checks source traceability, detects conflicting recommendations, and applies uncertainty quantification. Clinician-readable explanations accompany each answer.

Layer 4

Clinical Integration & Evaluation

Guideline Navigators are deployed in Electronic Health Record (EHR) environments at partner clinical sites. Prospective studies measure adherence outcomes, clinician trust, and workflow integration quality.
